# What to Look For in the Demo World

The prepared demo world is not just a flat test map. It is designed to help first-time testers notice the atmosphere, movement, and feedback cues that make TreasureRun feel like a small playable event rather than a static technical demo.

## UFO encounter

When the encounter begins, a UFO appears overhead, opens a beam of light, and lowers a wandering trader and two llamas to the ground.

Once they land, the UFO withdraws and disappears.

If the run times out, the departure sequence lifts the trader and llamas back up through the beam. They disappear beneath the UFO, as if the caravan has been pulled back into history.

## Moving safe zone

During the encounter, the area around the wandering trader and llamas becomes a moving safe zone. It feels warm, golden, and stable.

Outside that zone, the world feels colder and more fragile, with cracked ground and unstable visual cues.

As the trader and llamas move, they leave behind a yellow-to-amber trail, like an ancient path through history.

## Treasure proximity cues

The treasure hunt is guided by sound as well as sight.

As the player gets closer to treasure, heartbeat-like cues and cracking-ice sounds grow stronger, making the search feel more tense than a simple chest hunt.

## Outcome quotes

TreasureRun also includes 158 short encouragement quotes inspired by history, nature, and myth.

Instead of showing only plain win-or-lose messages, each run can add a small narrative layer to the treasure hunt.
